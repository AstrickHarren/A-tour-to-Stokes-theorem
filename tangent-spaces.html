Introduction to Mechanics on Manifolds

4 Tangent Spaces

Definition 4.1 (Smoothness in manifolds) A function \(f: U \to N\) defined on an open set of a manifold \(M\) is smooth at \(p \in U\) if there are charts \((V, \phi), (W, \psi)\) centered at \(p, f(p)\) resp. such that \[ \psi \circ f \circ \phi^{-1}: \phi(V \cap U \cap f^{-1}(W)) \to \mathbb R^n \] is smooth at \(p\) (in half spaces)

A function is smooth if it is smooth everywhere defined.

Proposition 4.1 Let \(M, N, R\) be three manifolds, and \(U, V\) open sets of \(M, N\) resp. If functions \[ f: U \to N, \quad g: V \to R \] are defined such that for some point \(p \in U\),

  1. \(f(p) \in V\)
  2. \(f\) is smooth at \(p\) and \(g\) is smooth at \(f(p)\)

Then \(g \circ f\) is smooth at \(p\).

Definition 4.2 (Germs) Suppose \(M\) is a manifold, and \(p \in M\). The germs of \(M\) at \(p\) are defined as equivalence classes specified on the set of all functionals defined on a neighborhood of \(p\) such that

  1. \(f \sim g\) if they agrees on a (smaller) neighborhood of \(p\).

The set of all germs of \(M\) at \(p\) is denoted as \(C^{\infty}_p(M)\).

Definition 4.3 In similar fashion, define \(C^\infty_p(M, N)\) as equivalance classes on the set of all smooth functions defined on a neighborhood of \(p \in M\) whose ranges are contained in \(N\) with the equivalence relation that

  1. \(f \sim g\) if they agrees on a (smaller) neighborhood of \(p\).

Proposition 4.2 The germs \(C^{\infty}_p(M)\) of a manifold at a point is a ring.

Definition 4.4 (Tangent vectors) A derivation or tangent vector at a point \(p \in M\) in a manifold is any function \(D: C_p^\infty(M) \to \mathbb R\) such that \[ D(fg) = (Df)g(p) + f(p)(Dg) \]

The set of all tangent vectors at \(p\) is called its tangent space, denoted by \(T_pM\).

Proposition 4.3 Tangent space at any point in any manifold is a vector space.

Definition 4.5 (Differential) Let \(F \in C^\infty_p(M, N)\) be a smooth function defined on an open subset of a manifold \(M\). If \(F\) is smooth at \(p\), then its differential at \(p\) is defined as the map \[ F_*: T_pM \to T_{f(p)}N \] such that \[ F_*(X)(f) = X(f \circ F) \] for all \(f \in C^\infty_{F(p)}(N)\).

Proposition 4.4 Differentials are linear transformations between tangent spaces.

4.1 Bases for Tangent Spaces

Definition 4.6 (Partial derivative) If \(M\) is a manifold, \(p \in M\) is a point, and \(\phi: U \to \mathbb R^n\) is a chart centered at \(p\). Write \[ \phi = (x^1, \dots, x^n) \] where \(x^i: U \to \mathbb R^n\). Define \[ \frac{\partial}{\partial x^i} \in T_pM \] with \[ \frac{\partial}{\partial x^i}f = \left . \frac{\partial(f \circ \phi^{-1})}{\partial r^i} \right \rvert_{\phi(p)} \] where the right hand side is the usual partial derivative of functions defined in euclidean spaces.

Theorem 4.2 Let \(M\) be a manifold, and \(\phi = (x^1, \dots, x^n)\) be a chart at \(p \in M\). The partial derivatives \[ \frac{\partial}{\partial x^1}, \quad \dots, \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial x^n} \] make up a basis for \(T_pM\).

Proof. It suffices to prove \(T\mathbb H^n\) has dimension \(n\), or that \(T\mathbb H^n \simeq R^n\). Proposition 4.5 If \(F \in C^\infty_p(M, N)\), then in local coordinates,

\[ F_*\left (\frac{\partial}{\partial x^i} \right) = \sum \frac{\partial F^i}{\partial x^j} \frac{\partial}{\partial y^i} \] where \(F^i = y^i \circ F\).

4.2 Tangent Bundle

Definition 4.7 (Tangent bundle) The disjoint union of all tangent spaces of a manifold is called its tangent bundle: \[ TM = \bigsqcup T_pM = \bigcup {p} \times T_pM \]

The following defines tangent bundle as a topological space.

  1. If \((U, \phi)\) is a chart of \(M\), define \[ TU = \bigcup_{p \in U} T_pM \]

  2. The function \(\bar\phi: TU \to \phi(U) \times \mathbb R^n\) defined with \[ \bar \phi(p, v) = (\phi(p), c^1, \dots, c^n) \] where \[ v = \sum c^i \left . \frac{\partial}{\partial x^i} \right \rvert_p \]

  3. Transfer the topology from \(U\) to \(TU\), making \(\bar \phi\) a homeomorphism

  4. Define the topology on \(TM\) using \[ \mathcal B = \{ B: \text{$B$ is open in $TU$ for some local coordinate $U \subset M$ } \} \] as its basis.

The following defines tangent bundle as a smooth manifold.

  1. If \(\{(U, \phi)\}\) is an atlas for \(M\), then \(\{TU, \bar \phi\}\) is an atlas for \(TM\).
